## Channel Selection Framework

### Three variables that determine channel fit

1. **ICP and audience concentration**: Where specific titles at specific firmographics actually spend time (LinkedIn, dev communities, niche Slack groups, industry events).
2. **ACV and sales motion**:
   - Low-touch PLG: sub-$1,000 ACV
   - Mid-market sales-assisted: $5,000–$50,000 ACV
   - Classic enterprise: $50,000+ ACV, multi-month cycles
3. **Message complexity and evaluation depth**: Simple tools with clear ROI → PLG, content, search. Complex platforms with change-management → outbound, events, partner-assisted sales.

### Health benchmarks
- **LTV:CAC**: Healthy ≥ 3:1, elite 4–6:1
- **CAC payback**: Target < 12 months blended
- **Average B2B SaaS CAC**: ~$1,200
- **Spend efficiency**: Top performers spend $1.0–$1.5 in S&M per $1 of new ARR; undisciplined companies spend ~$2.00 per $1 new ARR

| Channel | Typical CAC range (USD) | Notes |

## 1. Cold Email Outbound

### Best fit
- Mid-market and enterprise ACVs ($50K–$250K+)
- Narrow, well-defined ICPs with high-quality lists
- Sales-led or sales-assisted motions

### Poor fit
- Very low ACV PLG products (outbound CAC eclipses LTV)
- Broad SMB segments without clear targeting triggers

### Benchmarks
- Open rates: 38–42% average (target 40–60% for targeted SaaS outbound)
- Reply rates: 3–4% average; well-executed campaigns reach 8–15%
- Meeting booking rate: ~1% of total sends average; 1–3% for strong campaigns
- Meetings-to-opportunity: 25–40%
- Opportunity-to-close: 3–8%

### Playbook
1. **List design**: Narrow ICP by title, seniority, firm size, industry, tech stack. Prioritize lookalikes of best 50–100 customers. Bounce rate < 0.5%, spam complaints < 0.1%.
2. **Deliverability**: Dedicated sending domains, warm gradually. SPF, DKIM, DMARC. 30–75 sends/day per inbox.
3. **Sequencing**: 3–5 emails over 7–14 days. Text-only + 1–2 value-add (case study, Loom, benchmark). Personalize first lines with firmographic/trigger data.
4. **Routing**: CRM integration with disposition codes. SLAs for AE follow-up. Pair email with LinkedIn touches.
5. **Tooling**: Email finder/verification, sequencing tool, enrichment/intent data, CRM, deliverability monitoring.

## 2. LinkedIn Outbound

### Best fit
- Senior B2B decision-makers active on LinkedIn (89% use it for lead gen, 62% find it most effective)
- Higher ACV deals tolerating higher CAC for 3–10x better response rates vs email
- Founder/executive-led outreach (doubles response rates vs SDR-only)

### Poor fit
- ICP rarely on LinkedIn (blue-collar, field roles, certain geographies)
- Very high volume / very low CAC needs (sub-$1K ACV PLG)

### Benchmarks
- Connection acceptance: 30–40% good, 50%+ excellent, below 20% risks throttling
- Reply rates: 15–20%+ when personalization is strong
- Including a note: improves response rate (9.36% vs 5.44% in one analysis)

### Playbook
1. **Profile setup**: Optimize headline, banner, about for ICP. Post weekly thought leadership.
2. **Targeting**: Sales Navigator filters (title, seniority, function, industry, employee count, geography). Build micro-cohorts.
3. **Sequences**: Connection request → 2–4 follow-ups over 1–2 weeks referencing posts, shared groups, specific pains. Low-friction CTA.
4. **Safety**: Keep weekly connection volume within safe bands. Target 30–40%+ acceptance rate. Coordinate with email sequences.

## 3. Paid Search / PPC

### Best fit
- ICP actively searches for your category, competitors, or problem phrases
- Clear value prop expressible in ad copy + landing page
- ACV/margins support higher CAC ($800+ typical)

### Poor fit
- Tiny or ambiguous query volume (new categories)
- Can't differentiate on SERP or landing page

### Benchmarks
- CPC: $5–$15+ for B2B SaaS keywords
- CTR: 2–4% benchmark for search
- CPL: $50–$150 for sales-qualified leads
- CAC: $600–$1,200 (average ~$802 for B2B search)
- Lead-to-MQL: 34–41%
- MQL-to-SQL: 18–22%
- ROAS target: 4x+ revenue per ad dollar

### Playbook
1. **Tracking**: CRM integration, GCLID/UTM capture, conversion tracking for demos/trials + offline conversions.
2. **Keywords**: High-intent bottom-funnel terms ("software", "platform", "tool", "vendor") + competitor conquesting. Tight ad groups (5–20 keywords), strong negative lists.
3. **Creative/landing**: Multiple ad variations with differentiators + social proof. Landing pages aligned to keyword clusters.
4. **Optimization**: Weekly search query reports, bid adjustments. Monthly CAC, payback, ROAS evaluation.

## 4. SEO and Content Marketing

### Best fit
- B2B SaaS with searchable problems and self-educating buyers
- Mid-ticket ($1K–$20K ACV) where scalable inbound at modest CAC produces strong LTV:CAC
- Thought leadership and category creation matter

### Poor fit
- Very small, offline niches with limited search behavior
- Sales cycles driven by RFPs/procurement

### Benchmarks
- Organic CAC: $150–$480
- LTV:CAC: 5–8:1
- CAC payback: 8–14 months once engine built
- Healthy target: organic > 40% of total traffic
- AI overviews may reduce clicks ~30% while increasing impressions

### Playbook
1. **Strategy**: Map content to full funnel — problem-aware (how-to, comparisons), solution-aware ("best X for Y"), product-aware (feature, integration, case-study pages).
2. **Production**: 1–3 substantial posts/week. Mix blogs, comparisons, templates, calculators, benchmark pieces. Optimize on-page elements. Refresh older content.
3. **Distribution**: LinkedIn, email newsletters, partner co-marketing, communities. Repurpose into threads, webinars, sales enablement.

## 5. Communities and Events

### Best fit
- Roles valuing peer learning (RevOps, product, engineering, marketing leaders)
- Mid-market or enterprise ACV tolerating higher CAC
- Trust and social proof critical in vendor selection

### Impact data
- Companies with strong communities: 2.1x faster revenue growth, 46% higher LTV, ~32% lower acquisition costs, 29% lower churn

### Benchmarks
- Event CAC per new logo: $400–$500+
- Mature CLG motions can reduce blended CAC ~30% and increase LTV substantially

### Playbook
1. **Community design**: Member-first value prop (peer support, benchmarks, templates, roadmap influence). Platform choice (Slack, forum, dedicated tool).
2. **Programming**: Monthly AMAs, quarterly virtual events/roundtables, periodic cohort sessions. Monthly insight briefs from community conversations.
3. **Events**: Pre-book meetings with target accounts, host side events, follow up via outbound + retargeting. Capture engagement into CRM as intent signals.

## 6. Partnerships and Co-Marketing

### Best fit
- Product complementary to ICP's existing tools
- Agencies/consultants/SIs influence buying decisions

### Benchmarks
- Partner/referral CAC: $100–$150 (lowest across all channels)
- Constraint: scalability; lumpy deal flow, requires dedicated enablement

### Playbook
1. **Partner types**: Technology (integrations, marketplace listings), consulting/agency (implementation partners), co-marketing (non-competitive vendors, same ICP).
2. **Co-marketing execution**: Co-branded content gated on joint landing pages. Joint ABM plays against 20–50 shared target accounts.
3. **Enablement**: Partner tiers, benefits, requirements. Partner portal. Lead registration, attribution, commissions.

## 7. Product-Led Growth / Freemium

### Best fit
- Product delivers clear value quickly in self-serve (collaboration, analytics, workflow)
- ACV $1K–$20K with large user bases converting at reasonable rates

### Poor fit
- Heavy implementation / change management / multi-stakeholder sign-off
- Bespoke deals, security/regulatory concerns forcing high-touch sales

### Benchmarks
- 58% of B2B SaaS companies have a PLG motion
- Overall median free-to-paid conversion: ~9%
- $1K–$5K ACV products: ~10% conversion
- Card-required trials: ~30% conversion but far lower signup volume
- Freemium: 2–5% free-to-paid (much higher signups)
- Sales-assisted freemium: 5–7% average, 10–15% top performers
- Companies using PQLs see ~3x higher free-to-paid conversion (~25% for free trials with PQLs)
- Only ~25% of companies report using PQLs

### Playbook
1. **Packaging**: Choose motion (trial, freemium, hybrid) based on ACV + onboarding complexity. Free tier shows core value, reserves advanced features for paid.
2. **Onboarding**: Instrument activation events + PQLs. Guided onboarding to first value. Lifecycle messaging (in-app + email) for usage/upgrade prompts.
3. **Sales-assist**: Route high-intent PQLs to sales. Community + content for deeper engagement.

## 8. Paid Social (LinkedIn Ads, Meta Ads)

### Best fit
- Tight B2B targeting by job title, seniority, company size, industry
- Creating and capturing demand with upper/mid-funnel content

### Benchmarks — LinkedIn Ads (B2B SaaS, North America)
- CPC: $5–$8
- CTR: 0.4–0.6% median
- CPL: $115–$185
- Lead Gen Forms: reduce CPL ~25% (sometimes at cost of lead quality)
- Thought Leader Ads and document formats: often produce lowest CPL

### Benchmarks — Meta Ads
- Lower CPC and CAC (~$230 average CAC for some benchmarks)
- Weaker targeting precision and intent for B2B

### Blended paid social CAC: $400–$982 (LinkedIn at high end)

### Playbook
1. **Infrastructure**: LinkedIn Insight Tag / Meta pixel + server-side conversions APIs (can reduce CPA ~20%). CRM sync for revenue tracking.
2. **Audiences**: Tiered — broad (awareness), ICP-focused (consideration), account lists (ABM). Different offers per tier.
3. **Creative**: People-centric (founder/customer faces), social proof, short benefit-driven copy. Thought Leader Ads and documents for lowest CPL.
4. **Funnel**: Always-on retargeting for high-intent visitors. Thematic campaigns synced with outbound + content.

## Cross-Channel Strategy

### Budget allocation by stage

| Stage | Organic (content, SEO) | Paid (search, social) | Experimentation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Early (<$1M ARR) | 60–70% | 20–30% | 10–20% |
| Growth ($1M–$10M) | Diversify into outbound, events, partnerships | | |
| Scale ($10M+) | Full portfolio with disciplined CAC | | |

### Channel portfolio by ACV

**Low ACV / PLG-heavy**: PLG + SEO/content primary. Paid search + retargeting as support. Light outbound/co-marketing for higher-value segments only.

**Mid-market ACV**: SEO/content + paid search + sales-assisted PLG. Targeted outbound + LinkedIn Ads. Layer partnerships once ICP and proof points are clear.

**Enterprise ACV**: Outbound + events + ABM-style paid social + partnerships primary. High-authority content + selective PLG trials as support.

### Measurement governance
- Track channel-level CAC, CAC payback, LTV:CAC — not vanity metrics
- Red flags: payback > 12 months or LTV:CAC < 3:1 → trigger channel audit
- Coordinate "allbound" execution across paid, outbound, content, community
- Rebalance budgets quarterly based on net-new ARR contribution and CAC efficiency
- Regularly prune underperforming experiments
