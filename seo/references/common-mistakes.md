# Common SEO & AEO Mistakes

## Top 10 SEO Mistakes Early-Stage Startups Make

### 1. Writing for Google Instead of Humans
Keyword-stuffed content with no genuine insight doesn't rank in 2026 and doesn't convert. Signal: high impressions but low dwell time and high bounce. Fix: answer the searcher's actual question as completely and clearly as possible.

### 2. Overcomplicating Site Architecture
Five-level navigation for ten pages of content. Keep shallow (2 levels deep for small sites) until scale demands more.

### 3. Publishing Without a Keyword or Intent Map
Random blog publishing ("20 articles by interns with no keyword strategy, no internal linking, no understanding of intent") produces zero compounding SEO value. Every piece must map to a keyword, intent, funnel stage, and conversion pathway.

### 4. Targeting Head Terms Before Having Domain Authority
Seed-stage startup targeting "project management software" (KD 82) = no results for 2+ years. Target long-tail first; build authority; work upward. Head terms are earned, not chosen.

### 5. Ignoring Meta Tags and Image Optimization
Meta titles/descriptions are first brand touchpoint in SERP. Missing, duplicate, or auto-generated meta tags suppress CTR dramatically. Image alt text = accessibility requirement + SEO signal.

### 6. No SSL/HTTPS
Confirmed ranking signal and user trust prerequisite. Launching without it is an unforced error.

### 7. Buying or Exchanging Backlinks
Google's SpamBrain identifies unnatural link patterns. Manual penalties from purchased links are difficult to recover from. Every dollar on link purchase → better spent on digital PR or content.

### 8. Ignoring Google Search Console
The single most valuable free SEO tool. Many startups don't set it up for months, losing indexing data and missing manual action alerts.

### 9. Not Having a Conversion Path on Content Pages
Blog posts that rank but have no CTA, no email capture, no path to product = traffic with no business value. Every page needs a next step aligned to its funnel stage.

### 10. Expecting SEO Results in 30–60 Days
SEO operates on 3–12 month horizon. Teams that evaluate and abandon in first 90 days based on flat traffic are measuring wrong metric at wrong time. Track leading indicators (indexed pages, impressions, long-tail rankings) early — not organic sessions.

## AEO-Specific Mistakes

### Over-Optimization for AI Extraction
"AI-bait" — thin pages structured purely for extraction with no genuine depth. Will not sustain citation rates as AI engines improve quality assessment.

### Ignoring Traditional SEO in Favor of AEO
Some treat AEO as standalone channel. Wrong. AI engines (especially Google AI Overviews) primarily cite pages already ranking in traditional organic. You cannot win AEO without winning SEO first.

### Blocking AI Crawlers in robots.txt
Overly broad `Disallow: /` or outdated robots.txt blocking GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot. Makes content invisible to AI engines entirely.

### Inconsistent Entity Information Across Platforms
Company name spelled differently on website, Crunchbase, LinkedIn, Google Business Profile → AI models cannot confidently corroborate entity. May cite a competitor with consistent data instead.

## When SEO/AEO Is the Wrong Channel

SEO is WRONG as primary acquisition channel when:
- **Need revenue in next 90 days** — SEO takes 3–12 months. Use outbound, paid, or partnerships.
- **ICP searches with zero-volume queries** — if buyers use unique jargon with no measurable traffic, SEO opportunity is too small.
- **Product is genuinely novel with no search category** — category creation requires PR and thought leadership before SEO becomes viable.
- **Buying cycle requires deep relationship selling** — 18-month enterprise deals with multiple stakeholders won through relationships, not search.

**Signal that SEO IS worth prioritizing:** competitors have significant organic traffic, prospects use search to research category, 6+ months of runway to invest in compounding channel.
