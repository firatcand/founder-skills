# Link Building & Off-Page Authority

## Link Building Strategies (Priority Order)

### 1. Digital PR (Most Effective)
48.6% of SEO pros rate it #1 tactic (second consecutive year). Only 17.7% actually use it — opportunity gap.

**Assets that earn links:**
- **Original research** — survey of 100 customers, proprietary dataset analysis, benchmark report. Generates backlinks passively from journalists/bloggers citing data.
- **Contrarian takes** — well-evidenced argument against prevailing industry wisdom.
- **Free tools** — calculators, generators, graders, diagnostic tools on your domain. Natural backlinks from tool directories and bloggers.

### 2. HARO/Connectively
Journalist-source matching platform. Average quality backlinks from DR 50+ sites.

**Key mechanics:**
- Speed: pitches sent within first hour perform ~50% better
- Quality filter: ~80% of pitches rejected for being off-topic or promotional. Write for journalist's story, not your marketing.
- Website substance: journalists evaluate your site before linking — thin sites get passed over
- 43% of marketers use HARO as core link-building strategy

### 3. Resource Page Outreach
Find "top resources for [industry]" pages via `intitle:resources inurl:links site:.edu [topic]`. Pitch most authoritative content. Higher success rate than generic guest post outreach because page already exists for this purpose.

### 4. Unlinked Brand Mention Reclamation
Use Google Alerts or Ahrefs Alerts to find web mentions of your brand without a link. Reach out and request hyperlink. Conversion rate: 20–30%. Lowest-hanging fruit.

### 5. Guest Posting
Effectiveness has crashed as standalone strategy. Still viable when combined with genuine thought leadership and targeting relevant publications.

## Link Quality Criteria

93.8% of link builders now prioritize quality over quantity. Average quality backlink cost: $508 (2025), up from $364 (2023).

**What makes a link valuable:**
- **Topical relevance** — link from a site in your domain worth 5–10x a generic high-DR unrelated site
- **Domain Rating/Authority** — use as proxy but weight topical relevance above raw score
- **Real traffic** — link from site with genuine organic traffic = validated by Google
- **Contextual placement** — in-content editorial links outperform footer, sidebar, profile links
- **Follow vs. nofollow** — nofollow has indirect value (referral traffic, entity mentions) but doesn't pass PageRank directly

**Links to avoid (Google penalizes):**
- Purchased links or link network placements
- Private Blog Network (PBN) links
- Links from sites with no organic traffic and low content quality
- Reciprocal link schemes ("I'll link to you if you link to me")
- Footer/sitewide links across unrelated sites

## Internal Linking Architecture

One of the highest-leverage, lowest-cost SEO levers.

### Core Principles
1. Every cluster article MUST link back to its pillar page with consistent anchor text — foundational topical authority signal
2. High-authority pages should link to new/underperforming pages (immediately boosts crawl priority)
3. No orphan pages — every page needs at least 2–3 internal links pointing to it
4. Anchor text diversity: descriptive, keyword-relevant for important links; avoid over-optimizing with exact-match (manipulation risk)
5. Homepage links are gold — every core product/service page accessible from homepage in 1–2 clicks

Without bidirectional pillar-cluster links, you have silos that don't compound into topical authority.
